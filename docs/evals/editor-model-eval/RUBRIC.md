# Editor decision rubric — transcribed from the production editor prompts

This rubric is transcribed verbatim-in-substance from `agents/editor/SYSTEM.md`
and `agents/editor/INSTRUCTIONS.md` (the prompts that produced every arm's
output). It is the ONLY standard a judge applies. Judge the *editorial
decisions* on the contested slots — not writing polish.

## What the Editor is deciding
Which topic candidates become today's published multi-perspective dossiers, at
what priority, and why. The Editor argues **news value and contested framings**
— not the source landscape.

## Criteria (apply to each contested slot's accept/reject and its reasoning)

1. **Multi-perspective viability** — is the story contested across enough
   regions, languages, and stakeholder positions to support a dossier built
   around competing framings? A topic with narrow current breadth but sharp
   framing divergence between the parties may still qualify; one with broad
   coverage but thin substantive divergence may not. A single-region,
   single-position story almost never qualifies.

2. **News value / significance / stakes** — materiality and breadth of who is
   affected; transnational stakes; whether the decision or event carries broader
   implications.

3. **Novelty vs. previous coverage (follow-up discipline)** — a topic matching a
   prior package is an accept/high-priority ONLY on material new developments
   (new military action, policy reversal, differing verified casualty figures,
   ceasefire, a newly-existing official response). Additional outlets, longer
   pieces, or minor variation do NOT count — same story without new substance is
   a reject or deprioritize. A `follow_up_to` must point at a real prior package
   and name specific new developments.

4. **Rejection quality** — every reject names what the topic lacks as a judgment
   about the topic itself (narrow scope, single-region, no contested framings,
   no broader implications, repetition without new substance). "Not important"
   is not a reason.

5. **Reason hygiene** — `selection_reason` argues significance and the existence
   of contested framings. An actor's position is allowed ("the government's
   framing", "Tehran's account"); a source's origin is NOT ("European outlets",
   "German-language coverage", named outlets, source/region/language counts).
   2-4 sentences, mandatory for accepts and rejects alike.

6. **Priority discipline** — accepts spread across the 1-10 range (clustering at
   the top = failing discrimination); no more than two accepts share a priority;
   the daily set is not dominated by a single domain or region.

7. **Sport rule** — sport topics qualify only through stakes beyond the sport
   (governance, labor, public spending, health policy, geopolitics). Match
   results, standings, tournament progression, athletic performance are by
   themselves rejects.

## The judgment
For each contested slot, decide which arm(s) made the sounder editorial call
(accept vs reject, and the quality of the stated reasoning) under criteria 1-7.
Then, for the day overall, name the arm whose selected set + reasoning is
strongest on the contested slots. Ties are allowed. You are judging editorial
judgment, not prose.
