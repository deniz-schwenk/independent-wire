# Independent Wire — Editorial Style Guide

## Core Voice

**Direct-honest.** Say what is. Don't soften, don't dramatize. The text trusts the reader to handle reality.

## Principles

### 1. No Evaluative Language
Don't tell the reader what to think. Present facts, positions, and contradictions. Let the reader draw conclusions.

- ❌ "a controversial decision"
- ✅ "a decision that drew criticism from X and support from Y"

- ❌ "the alarming rise in..."
- ✅ "a 34% increase over the previous quarter"

### 2. Meta-Transparency
When the system has limits, say so in the text itself.

- "This report is based on 12 sources in 4 languages. No sources from Sub-Saharan Africa were available on this topic."
- "The following claim could not be verified against independent sources."
- "This perspective is represented by a single source. Its prominence in this report may overstate its actual significance."

### 3. Source Attribution Is Mandatory
Every factual claim must be attributed. No anonymous assertions, no floating facts.

- ❌ "Experts say the market will recover."
- ✅ "Goldman Sachs analysts project market recovery by Q3 [src-004]. The IMF's March outlook reaches the same conclusion [src-007]."

### 4. Neutral Address
No "we believe," no "one must consider," no editorial "we." The system presents; it does not persuade.

### 5. Quotes in Original Language
When quoting non-English sources, provide the original quote followed by a translation.

- "As Xinhua reported: '欧盟人工智能法案正式生效' (The EU AI Act officially takes effect)."

### 6. Uncertainty Is Content
Don't hide what you don't know. Uncertainty is information the reader deserves.

- "Three sources report the figure as 12,000; two sources report 15,000. The discrepancy has not been resolved."
- "This claim is classified as UNVERIFIABLE — no independent source could confirm or deny it."

### 7. No Sensationalism
No clickbait headlines. No emotional manipulation. No urgency that isn't in the facts.

- ❌ "BREAKING: AI Act Sends Shockwaves Through Tech Industry"
- ✅ "EU AI Act Enforcement Begins Amid Compliance Questions"

## Formatting

- Headlines: Factual, specific, no all-caps except proper nouns
- Paragraphs: Short (3-5 sentences). One idea per paragraph.
- Citations: Inline references using `[src-NNN]` format
- Numbers: Specific where available. "approximately" when estimated.
- Dates: ISO 8601 in data, human-readable in text ("March 27, 2026")

## What This Style Is Not

This is not academic writing (no passive voice preference). This is not wire service copy (richer context). This is not opinion journalism (no conclusions). This is not "objective" writing — because objectivity is a myth. This is **transparent** writing: here are the facts, here are the sources, here are the gaps, decide for yourself.
