Pope Leo XIV Concludes Spain Visit With Condemnation of People Smugglers and Call for Legal Migration Routes

The pontiff's final day in the Canary Islands coincided with the EU Migration and Asylum Pact taking effect, drawing competing claims from Spanish political parties over alignment with his message.

Pope Leo XIV ended his visit to Spain on June 12 with a direct warning to human traffickers and a call for Europe to create safe, legal migration pathways, before a technical fault grounded his return flight and forced him to leave on a Falcon jet provided by King Felipe VI [1][2][17].

Speaking at the Las Raíces migrant reception center in Tenerife, the Pope told smugglers: "Stop. Repent. For every life lost, every family deceived … you will have to appear before divine justice" [1].

The pontiff also described smuggling networks as "monsters" and mafias that trade on the desperation of migrants, and warned that Europe and the Mediterranean must not become "cemeteries without tombstones" [13][15]. He called on global leaders to establish safe, legal migration routes and robust rescue operations, and urged an "examination of conscience" on how the world responds to the migration crisis [10][11].

The visit's final day coincided with the EU Migration and Asylum Pact entering into force, a backdrop that multiple outlets noted sharpened the political resonance of the Pope's remarks [3][9]. Human rights groups have warned the pact could lead to increased deportations [6].

Alongside his condemnation of traffickers, the Pope articulated a reciprocal framework for integration, stating that migrants should learn the language and respect the laws of their host country while host societies must in turn welcome and dignify newcomers [3][9]. "El extranjero de ayer puede ser el hermano y vecino de hoy" — the foreigner of yesterday can be the brother and neighbor of today — he told those gathered at Las Raíces [3].

The Pope also explicitly rejected the "national priority" immigration agenda promoted by Spain's right-wing Popular Party (PP) and far-right Vox party, defending equal dignity for all people regardless of origin [8]. Despite this, PP spokesperson Ester Muñoz stated that her party was "completely in agreement" with the Pope's words and that its national-priority policy was compatible with his message, while Vox leader Santiago Abascal applauded the speech and denied any contradiction with his party's position [20]. The Spanish government, by contrast, praised the Pope's address as "valiant" [20].

The Atlantic migration route from Senegal and Mauritania to the Canary Islands remained a central reference point throughout the visit. NGO Caminando Fronteras recorded more than 3,000 deaths on the route last year and had called on the Pope ahead of his arrival to combat the dehumanization and invisibilization of migrants [1][19]. Migrant Bousso Diouf, speaking during the visit, said migrants seek not special privileges but "respect, humanity and the opportunity to live with dignity" [1].

Volunteers, pilgrims, and clergy who attended events during the visit described the experience in primarily spiritual terms. Pilgrim Lola, from Granada, thanked the Pope for "not mixing political concepts" with his message of faith and unity [4]. Several priests from Guadalajara recalled his call to build "a culture of encounter rather than polarization" [4].

The return journey was disrupted when a technical fault grounded the Iberia Airbus 320 that had been scheduled to fly the Pope from Tenerife to Rome, forcing him to return to the terminal [2][17]. King Felipe VI, who had already departed, returned to the airport, boarded the grounded aircraft to accompany the Pope, and ultimately offered his personal Falcon jet for the return flight to Rome [2].

Divergence types

factual
Sources disagree on a verifiable fact: a date, number, name, or whether something happened.

Sources describe the same event using different language or implied meaning. Example: one outlet calls a payment “compensation,” another calls it “sanctions relief.”
omission
One or more sources report something that other sources leave out entirely.
emphasis
Sources cover the same event but give different aspects different weight or prominence. Example: one outlet leads with casualty figures; another treats them as a footnote to the political negotiations.

Bias issues

A descriptive word that signals the writer’s judgment rather than a neutral fact. Examples: “staggering,” “sharp,” “dramatic.”

Vocabulary carrying strong political or emotional connotations that a more neutral word would avoid. Examples: “regime” vs. “government,” “crackdown” vs. “enforcement.”
