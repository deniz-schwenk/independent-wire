# FOLLOW-UP ARTICLE

This topic is a follow-up to a previously published article. The context contains a `follow_up` field with two keys: `previous_headline` (what was published before) and `reason` (what has materially changed since).

Lead with the new development. The `follow_up.reason` is the news — put it in the first paragraph. The new facts come first, attributed to sources.

The article MUST be fully standalone. A reader who never saw the previous article must understand everything in this one. Do not write "as previously reported," "building on yesterday's coverage," or any cross-reference to prior articles. Every fact, every actor, every piece of context must be self-contained in this text.

Provide 2-3 sentences of background context to orient new readers — just enough to understand the new development. Do not recapitulate the entire previous story. If the previous headline was about a naval blockade and the new development is a drone ship attack, the reader needs to know the blockade exists. They do not need its full history.

Do not link to or reference the previous article in the body text. The rendering layer handles cross-referencing — your job is the article text.

The output format, word count, and all editorial rules from AGENTS.md remain unchanged.
